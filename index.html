Indian poultry industry suffered huge crisis in early 1980s, as egg selling price has fallen down than the production cost.  Due to this crisis, poultry farmers shut down their poultry farming operation due to heavy loss.

Group of poultry farmers supported by Dr. B. V. Rao Ji, Ch. Jagapati Rao Ji and others travelled across the county to meet poultry farmers and traders, organizing over 300 meetings. Their main Objective – to unite poultry farmers from all over India with the slogan of “My EGG My Price”.  After massive effort, poultry farmers united to create a united organization to facilitate poultry farmers and traders in determining the egg rate. In May 1982, NECC was formally registered and started to declare egg prices.  NECC also called as National Egg Coordination Committee, played significant role for the betterment of poultry industry, by staying connected with poultry farmers, conducting various service like support operations, consumer education, egg promotion campaigns, marketing and research, rural marketing development, etc…

Factors Determining the Egg Rate:

Wholesale price of the egg varies based on the market demand, supply and production. Here are some important factors that determine the egg rates in India.

Bird Flu Outbreak: Egg price and demand get sharp difference due to bird flu outbreak. It may affect egg price for weeks.

Pandemic: Due to lockdown and other restriction across several states, the demand for egg has reduced.

Festive Season: Egg consumption of egg lower during the festival season. For Example – In Tamilnadu, most of people avoid consumption of non-veg foods. Hence, the daily consumption of egg decreases.

Summer Season: Due to lower consumption of poultry products during summer, the egg price gets affected.

Christmas Season: Egg price are high during the November and December, as the demand increases during Christmas.  Eggs are used in large quantity for cake production during Christmas and New Year.

School Reopening: Egg rate increases during June and July due to school opening timings. Most state serve egg to students as part of their afternoon meal scheme.

Namakkal Egg Market:

Namakkal is one of the major poultry products in India. It is considered as largest supplier across South India. About 65% of total egg production in Tamilnadu comes from Namakkal. Poultry products like egg, broiler chicken and hatching eggs are exported to neighboring states like Kerala, Karnataka, Andhra Pradesh, Telangana, Maharashtra and other northern states. The demand for Namakkal eggs keep on increasing for its quality and taste.

 Namakkal daily egg rates are fixed by the NECC (National Egg Coordination Committee) on daily basis. Egg price in Kerala, Karnataka and other part of South India is determined based on Namakkal egg rate.

Barwala Egg Rate:

Barwala is another popular poultry hub in India, located in Haryana. The daily egg production capacity Barwala is 1 crores.  There are about 200+ poultry farms having 8 million birds and over 1500 workers engage in daily jobs.

Egg produced from Barwala farms are consumed in Punjab, Haryana, Delhi, Uttar Pradesh, Delhi, Bihar, West Bengal, Himachal Pradesh and other states. Barwala egg rate is determined by the NECC (National Egg Coordination Committee) on regular basis. As mentioned above, egg rates are affected based on the demand and supply.
